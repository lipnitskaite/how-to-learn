The main challenges in learning

Neither at school nor at the institute do they teach us how to study the material properly. We prepare for exams and study the tickets. We practice solving monotonous problems in order to pass the test better, but often in the end this does not give us real knowledge. Memorisation quickly wears off and is not useful.

Conclusion: You have to be able to learn, but for some reason, not many schools teach it. What can we do about it?

Specific techniques and exercises help to change the approach to learning, making it effective and exciting. The same techniques are applied to the Practicum learning example.

Training Techniques

Five practices from Barbara Oakley

  • Two types of attention

    Deeper knowledge arises by alternating between focused and scattered thinking.

  • Recall

    Recall what you've learnt - this will help connect the disparate portions of your memory.

  • Interliving

    Learn several skills at the same time, they enrich each other.

  • Questions

    As you listen to the instructor, think of a good question that you really care about.

  • The illusion of competence

    Repeat to yourself, write it down, tell a friend: you only think you have mastery of a new topic.

For those who like to procrastinate

Barbara Oakley's story.

From childhood, Barbara had a problem with maths. She considered herself a complete humanitarian, and not the smartest. In the army, she studied Russian to get a bonus, and was so successful that she was promoted to commander. But to get promoted, she had to pass maths. So Barbara came up with her own approach to the exact sciences. It turned out that if you are "bad at something", your hard-won knowledge is much deeper than that of those to whom everything is clear at a glance.
